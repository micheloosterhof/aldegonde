## Option 1: Sell Now (Private Sale) & Buy Replacement

### What You Get

- **Sale price estimate:** S$30,000-40,000 (based on similar 2017 Foresters on Carousell/SGCarMart with ~1 year COE remaining)
- The buyer gets your remaining COE + PARF eligibility
- You receive cash from the sale

### Pros
- Highest total cash recovery (sale price > scrap value + rebates)
- Buyer pays a premium over scrap value because they get the remaining COE
- Flexibility to buy any replacement immediately

### Cons
- Hassle of private sale (viewings, negotiations, paperwork)
- Need replacement car immediately (or go carless)
- Market timing risk - prices for used cars with short COE fluctuate

### Best For
- Maximising financial return from current car
- Those who don't mind the effort of selling privately

## Option 2: Deregister & Scrap, Then Buy New

### Rebates You Collect

| Rebate | Amount | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| PARF Rebate | S$7,845 | 50% of ARF (must deregister before 17 Feb 2027) |
| COE Rebate | ~S$4,841 | Pro-rated: ~11 months remaining of S$52,807 over 120 months |
| **Total Rebates** | **~S$12,686** | Can offset new car registration fees |

**Warning: The COE rebate shrinks by ~S$440 every month you wait.** Deregistering in Jan 2027 (1 month remaining) yields only ~S$440 in COE rebate vs ~S$4,841 now. Act early to maximise rebates.

### Sub-Option 2A: Buy New Electric Vehicle (EV)

**Example: BYD Atto 3 (Cat A version, 100kW)**

| Cost Item | Amount |
|-----------|--------|

**10-Year Total Cost of Ownership (TCO):**

| Annual Cost | Amount | 10-Year Total |
|-------------|--------|---------------|
| Depreciation (car value / 10yr) | ~S$12,000-13,000/yr | S$120,000-130,000 |
| Road tax (EV, ~110kW) | ~S$742/yr | S$7,420 |
| Insurance | ~S$2,000-3,000/yr | S$20,000-30,000 |
| Electricity (20,000km/yr, ~16kWh/100km) | ~S$1,250-1,560/yr | S$12,500-15,600 |
| Maintenance (minimal - no oil changes) | ~S$500/yr | S$5,000 |
| Parking/ERP | ~S$2,400/yr | S$24,000 |
| **Total 10-year TCO** | | **~S$189,000-212,000** |

**Advantages:**
- Lowest fuel costs (~S$100-130/month vs S$250-300 for petrol)
- Government rebates of up to S$45,000 (VES + EEAI until Dec 2026)
- Lowest road tax
- Minimal maintenance (no engine oil, no transmission service)
- 10 fresh years of COE
- No road tax age surcharge

**Disadvantages:**
- Higher insurance premiums (S$2,000-5,000/yr vs ~S$800-1,000)
- Battery degradation risk (replacement S$10,000-15,000+)
- Charging infrastructure dependency
- Higher upfront capital even after rebates
- Home charging requires HDB/condo charger access

### COE Renewal: Pros & Cons

**Advantages:**
- No large upfront capital for new car
- Keep a familiar, paid-off car
- Lower insurance premiums than new car
- No new car depreciation hit

**Disadvantages:**
- PARF rebate forfeited (S$7,845)
- Escalating road tax surcharges (up to +50%)
- Rising maintenance costs on aging vehicle
- 5-year renewal: car MUST be scrapped at end (no further renewal)
- No resale value at end
- Opportunity cost of S$63k-126k tied up in an aging car
- Government policy increasingly unfriendly to older ICE cars

## Option 4: Go Carless (Sell/Scrap, Don't Replace)

### What It Costs

| Cost Item | Annual | 10-Year Total |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| Grab/taxi (est. 3-4 rides/day) | ~S$9,000-15,000 | S$90,000-150,000 |
| MRT/bus pass | ~S$1,200-1,500 | S$12,000-15,000 |
| Occasional car rental (weekends/trips) | ~S$3,000-5,000 | S$30,000-50,000 |
| **Total transport cost** | **~S$13,000-21,500/yr** | **~S$132,000-215,000** |

Plus you collect S$12,686 in rebates (or ~S$35k if sold privately).

### Pros
- Potentially lowest total transport cost
- No capital tied up in a depreciating asset
- No insurance, road tax, parking, maintenance hassle
- Invest the S$63k-130k you would have spent — at 4% that's S$2,500-5,200/yr

### Cons
- Loss of convenience and flexibility (especially with children, groceries, late nights)
- Grab surge pricing can be unpredictable
- No car for spontaneous trips or emergencies
- Cultural/lifestyle adjustment

### Best For
- Households with good MRT access and no young children
- Those who drive infrequently (<10,000 km/year)
- People who want to free up capital

## Option 5: Let COE Expire & Then Buy New

### What Happens

- Car becomes illegal to drive after 17 Feb 2027
- You **forfeit ALL rebates** (both PARF S$7,845 and COE rebate ~S$4,841)
- Total forfeited: **~S$12,686**
- Car is deregistered and scrapped automatically

### Why This Is Almost Always the Worst Option

- You lose S$12,686 in rebates for no reason
- Same outcome as Option 2 but S$12,686 poorer
- Only makes sense if you genuinely cannot act before expiry

**This is a dominated option — every other option is strictly better. Avoid.**

## Recommendation Matrix

### If your priority is **lowest total cost over 10 years (and you need a car):**
**Sell privately + buy new EV (BYD Atto 3 / Dolphin class)**
- Private sale recovers ~S$35k (vs S$12.7k from scrapping)
- Government rebates slash EV upfront cost
- Lowest fuel and maintenance
- 10-year TCO: ~S$154,000-177,000

### If your priority is **lowest total cost (and you can live without a car):**
**Go carless (sell privately)**
- Pocket ~S$35k from sale
- Use Grab, MRT, occasional rental
- 10-year transport cost: ~S$97,000-180,000 (depends heavily on usage)

### If your priority is **lowest upfront cash outlay (but still need a car):**
**Renew COE 5 years**
- Only ~S$63k upfront
- But you face COE price risk in 5 years when you MUST buy a new car
- 10-year total is likely the most expensive path (~S$236k+)

### If your priority is **familiarity and convenience:**
**Renew COE 10 years**
- Keep your car, no hassle
- But ~S$274k-299k over 10 years — comparable to buying new
- Rising maintenance risk on a 15-20 year old boxer engine

### If your priority is **maximum cash recovery from current car:**
**Sell privately NOW (before COE shrinks further)**
- Private sale yields ~S$35k vs S$12.7k from scrap
- Every month you wait, the buyer's remaining COE drops and your sale price falls

### If you value **balance of cost, reliability, and flexibility:**
**Deregister + buy new hybrid**
- Moderate fuel savings without charging dependency
- Toyota reliability
- No charging infrastructure concerns
- Collect S$12,686 in rebates

## Key Deadlines

| Date | Action Required |
|------|----------------|
| **Now - Dec 2026** | Best window to sell privately (buyer still gets meaningful COE) |
| **Before 17 Feb 2027** | Must deregister to collect PARF (S$7,845) + COE rebate (~S$4,841) |
| **Before 17 Feb 2027** | Must submit COE renewal application if renewing |
| **17 Feb - 17 Mar 2027** | Late renewal window (with penalty fee) |
| **After 17 Mar 2027** | Car deregistered, all rebates forfeited |
| **31 Dec 2026** | EV Early Adopter Incentive (EEAI) expires - get EV before this! |

## Action Items

1. **Check exact road tax** on [OneMotoring](https://vrl.lta.gov.sg/lta/vrl/action/pubfunc?ID=EnquireRoadTaxByEngCap) using your plate number
2. **Get private sale quotes** from SGCarMart, Carousell, or dealers to understand your car's market value
3. **Test drive EVs** - BYD Atto 3, BYD Dolphin, or Hyundai Kona Electric
4. **Check home charging feasibility** - does your HDB/condo have EV charger access?
5. **Compare insurance quotes** for your shortlisted replacement cars
6. **Act before 31 Dec 2026** if going EV (to capture EEAI rebate)
7. **Act before 17 Feb 2027** regardless of decision (to avoid forfeiting rebates)
