# Characterization: Doublet Spacing is Poisson, Not Mathematical

## Claim

The 89 ciphertext doublets are positioned as a **homogeneous Poisson process** —
random, memoryless, content-driven. Their positions and gaps have **no link to
any mathematical series** (primes, totient, Fibonacci, lattice). This disproves
the class of mechanisms that would place doublets at computable positions.

## Status

**Status**: confirmed (characterization)

This is not a cipher hypothesis but a measured property of the doublet point
process. It is a constraint every cipher hypothesis must respect, and it rules
out the math-series-positional class outright.

## What was measured

Doublet = position i where C[i] == C[i+1]. 89 doublets in 13,136 runes,
rate 0.00678, mean spacing 147.6.

**Gap distribution** (88 gaps): min 6, max 712, mean 147.1, median 109,
std 141.5.
- Coefficient of variation std/mean = 0.962 (geometric/exponential -> 1.0).
- Median = mean x ln2 to within rounding (102 vs 109) — exponential right-skew.
- KS test vs exponential(mean 148): D=0.071, **p=0.75** — cannot reject
  exponential.

**Dispersion** (Fano factor = var/mean of per-bin counts; Poisson -> 1.0):
0.91 / 1.02 / 1.04 / 1.09 across bin sizes 50–400. Flat at 1.0.

**Periodicity** (DFT of the indicator): max-peak / mean-power = 9.89, which is
the white-noise expectation (~ln(n/2) = 8.8). No real period. The peaks sit at
random short periods, not a key length.

## Math-series rule-outs

Positions and gaps were tested against every series Cicada favors:

| test | observed | expected | verdict |
|------|----------|----------|---------|
| positions that are prime | 7 | 10.6 | none (~1σ low) |
| i+1 prime / i−1 prime | 4 / 12 | 10.6 / 10.6 | inconsistent -> noise |
| positions Fibonacci | 0 | 0.14 | none |
| positions square | 2 | 0.78 | none |
| GCD of all gaps | 1 | — | not on a lattice |
| gaps mod 29 uniformity | chi-sq 30.0 | <41 crit | uniform |
| phi(i) mod 29 at doublets | 13.19 | 13.74 overall | identical, no link |

The scattered prime results (i−1 high, i+1 low) are inconsistent in sign and
are multiple-comparison noise, not a prime effect.

This complements `running-key-math-sequence.md`, which already disproved
math-sequence **keystreams** (primes, totient, Fibonacci, squares, etc., both
Beaufort and Vigenere, all offsets) by the IOC test. Positions, gaps, and
keystreams are all now ruled out for mathematical series.

## The one micro-anomaly

Zero gaps below 6 (no two doublets within 5 runes); ~2.9 expected under a pure
exponential. A ~1.5σ avoidance of close doublets — the only deviation from pure
randomness. Consistent with a trigger that rarely occurs twice in quick
succession (a single rare rune, or a pattern that avoids near-repeats). Weak;
worth keeping in mind, not strong enough to build on.

## Implications

- The doublets **track the plaintext at random positions** — exactly what a rare
  plaintext rune or fixed n-gram occurring at a constant ~0.68% rate produces.
  This supports the "a plaintext pattern leaks through" framing.
- **Ruled out**: any mechanism that places doublets by position — periodic keys,
  prime/totient/Fibonacci position rules, lattice/arithmetic-progression
  placement. The positions are not generated by a formula.
- Poisson spacing does **not** discriminate a single-rune trigger from a
  fixed-sequence (n-gram) trigger; both yield identical exponential gaps.

Confirmed characterization. Doublets are a content-driven Poisson process with
no mathematical structure in their positions or gaps. Math-series positional
mechanisms are disproved; the trigger is whatever rare plaintext event the
cipher leaks, located wherever it falls in the text.
